# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CommandSynergy is for Magic: The Gathering Commander players working through focused deckbuilding sessions. They are assembling and refining a list, comparing candidate cards, and checking whether a deck lands in the bracket and power band they want before they commit to changes.

## Product Purpose

CommandSynergy is a deckbuilding workspace and analysis tool that helps players search cards, validate commander legality, inspect deck composition, and review bracket and synergy output in one place. Success means users can move from rough idea to trustworthy deck decisions without bouncing between disconnected tools or losing confidence in the rules and analysis.

## Brand Personality

The product should feel bold, mechanical, and opinionated. It should communicate taste and confidence rather than generic utility, balancing tactile tabletop energy with the precision of a serious deck analysis instrument. The visual direction should stay light-first, archival, and strategically sharp rather than glossy, futuristic, or sterile.

## Anti-references

Do not let the interface drift into generic SaaS or admin dashboard patterns. Avoid glossy futuristic styling, neutral dashboard chrome, filler surfaces, or generic productivity-tool cues that make deckbuilding feel like clerical data entry instead of a deliberate, collectible, high-signal craft.

## Design Principles

- Make deckbuilding feel deliberate and consequential, not like filling out a form.
- Pair tactile warmth with analytical clarity so the UI feels both collectible and trustworthy.
- Use visual hierarchy to foreground decisions, bracket signals, and deck-state changes quickly.
- Be opinionated in presentation: surface what matters, reduce neutral filler, and avoid generic admin patterns.
- Keep the workspace feeling like a crafted analysis instrument rather than a generic dashboard shell.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA expectations across the workspace. Preserve strong keyboard and screen reader support for analysis, search, and editing flows, and keep motion restrained or reducible so it never interferes with comprehension or control.
