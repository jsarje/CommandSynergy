## Design Context

### Users
CommandSynergy is for Magic: The Gathering players who are crafting their ideal Commander deck. The interface matters most during focused deckbuilding sessions where users are assembling a list, comparing options, and checking whether the deck lands in the bracket and power band they want.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel warm, mechanical, and opinionated. It should communicate taste and confidence rather than generic utility, balancing tactile tabletop energy with the precision of a serious deck analysis tool.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-first interface that feels like a refined deckbuilding workbench: archival, tactile, and strategically sharp rather than glossy or futuristic. The experience should avoid generic SaaS/dashboard cues and instead feel closer to a crafted analysis instrument for Commander players. There are no explicit visual references to mimic.

### Design Principles
- Make deckbuilding feel deliberate and consequential, not like filling out a form.
- Pair tactile warmth with analytical clarity so the UI feels both collectible and trustworthy.
- Use visual hierarchy to foreground decisions, bracket signals, and deck-state changes quickly.
- Be opinionated in presentation: surface what matters, reduce neutral filler, and avoid generic admin patterns.
- Meet WCAG AA expectations and keep motion expressive only when it does not obstruct clarity or control.
